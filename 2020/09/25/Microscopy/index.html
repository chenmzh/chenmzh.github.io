Module 3A: Constructing a compound epi-fluorescence microscope

Knowledge point


Refraction:

What’s a refraction?
Refraction is the change in direction of wave propogation due to a change in its transmission medium

Snell’s Law:

$$ \frac{sin\beta}{sin\alpha} = \frac{n_1}{n_2} $$

$\beta$ is the angle of refraction and $\alpha$ is the angle of incident light.

$n_1$ is the refractive index in the side of incident light, and $n_2$ is the refractive index in the side of refracted light

There will be a reflected light with the same angle of $\alpha$ at the side of $n_1$ medium

3 Ray tracing laws:

  • Parallel incident rays converge at the same point in the back focal plan of the lens
  • Rays originating from the same point on the front focal plane exit the lens parallel
  • Rays passing the lens center as if unaffected


Image Formation

One len system

Thin lens equation:
$$\frac{1}{s_i}+\frac{1}{s_o}=\frac{1}{f}$$

$s_i$ is the distance of image.
$s_o$ is the distance of object.
$f$ is the focal distance.

The single len system is a finite conjugate imaging system(order objectives), and called finite conjugate imaging

Magnification:
$$M = -\frac{s_i}{s_o}=\frac{f}{f-s_o}$$

Two lens system

Image formation with multiple lenses: infinite conjugate imaging (modern objective) (compound microscope)

Magnification:

$$M = -\frac{s_i}{s_o}=\frac{f_t}{f_o}$$

$f_t$ is the focal distance of tube len
$f_o$ is the focal distance of objective len


Critical illumination and Kohler illumination

Critical illumination:

Light source plane is conjugated with the object plane. Which made the image of light source overlap with the object.

Structure:

Source plane - collective lens - aperture iris - condenser lens - object plane - objective lens - objective back focal plane - tube lens - final image plane

Pros:

  • Maximum possible light intensity at sample

Cons:

  • Light source image overlap with the object.
Kohler illumination

Light source is conjugated with the aperture iris plane and the light is spread evenly over the object plane

Structure:

Source plane - collective lens - field iris - field lens - aperture iris - condenser lens - object plane - objective lens - objective back focal plane - tube lens - final image plane

Pros:

  • No image overlap

Cons:

  • The strength of light will be lower


Objective lens

Important Parameters:

NA: Numerical Aperture

$$NA = n_{media} * sin(\theta)$$

  • Higher NA, Maximum intensity in an image of a point object is more sensitive to the thickness error of coverslip
  • Generally, higher NA has shorter Working Distance and Wider lens

By changing the $n_{media}$, NA can reach ~1.5. $sin(\theta)$ can reach 0.95

$$\infty/0.14 \quad WD \: 0.21$$

$\infty$ is the tube length

$0.14$ is the coverslip thickness, means 0.14 mm

$WD \: 0.21$ is the working distance from the top of the object length to the sample. Working Distance is Not the focal distance. Unit: mm

WD corresponds to the maximum imaging depth possible.
